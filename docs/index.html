Welcome to the World of Musicals!

Hi! I’m Grace, and my love for musicals has been a journey filled with unforgettable moments. My first encounter with musicals was in third grade, when my mom enrolled me in a musical camp. There, a professional musical actress taught us songs, dances, and acting. We performed numbers from FAME, which became my first hands-on experience with the magical world of musicals. Around the same time, I had another eye-opening experience during a family trip to New York. I had the chance to watch The Lion King on Broadway, and I was completely mesmerized by the stunning visuals, elaborate costumes, and beautiful music. Even as a child, I felt the magic and grandeur of live theater.

A few years later, in seventh grade, I was given a school assignment to explore arts and culture. For this, I watched Napoleon, a lesser-known musical that left a modest impression on me. At the time, I thought my connection to musicals ended there.

Years later, during the COVID-19 pandemic, I stumbled upon a YouTube video of someone creatively covering Chicago's "Cell Block Tango." The performer added her own flair, emphasizing the individual stories of the murderesses, which reignited my curiosity. This led me to rediscover Chicago, which became the first musical I truly wanted to watch.

In high school, I began learning about classics like Rent and Les Misérables. However, my passion truly blossomed after my high school graduation, when I traveled to Korea and saw the Death Note musical. That experience turned me into a dedicated musical fan, especially after discovering actor Kim Sung Cheol. I soon started attending various performances, including Harlan County and the Broadway tour of Chicago. I even invested in opera glasses and binoculars to enhance my experience.

On a return trip to Korea during the winter, I watched Monte Cristo, Il Tenore, Rebecca, and more. Back in the U.S., my passion persisted as I saw Six and Wicked. Now, I’m eagerly looking forward to Kinky Boots, Jekyll and Hyde, Frankenstein, and Hadestown. Musicals have shaped my creativity and perspective, and I can’t wait to see where this passion takes me next.
